Homemade Sushi

Homemade Sushi Rolls are a versatile recipe that can be filled with any ingredients you choose. The prep time is roughly 40 minutes, with a cook time of 25 minutes. This recipe will yield 4 rolls that can be cut into as many, or as little pieces you would like. We recommend serving with tamari sauce, or dark soy sauce.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/3 cups water
  • 2/3 cup uncooked short-grain white rice
  • 3 tablespoons rice
  • 3 tablespoons white sugar
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 4 sheets nori seaweed sheets
  • 1/2 pound imitation crabmeat - This can be substituted with sushi salmon, or teriyaki chicken, for example
  • 1 avocado - peeled, pitted, and sliced
  • 1/2 cucumber - peeled, cut into small strips
  • 2 tablespoons pickled ginger

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 150 degrees C (300 degrees F).
  2. Cook the short-grain white rice in a rice cooker. Alternatively, cook rice on the stove top until the water has been absorbed.
  3. Mix rice vinegar, sugar, and salt in a small bowl. Gently stir into cooked rice in the pot and set aside.
  4. Lay nori sheets on a baking sheet, and heat the nori in the preheated oven until warm for 1-2 minutes.
  5. Center 1 nori sheet on a bamboo sushi mat (alternatively use a clean dish towel). Use wet hands to spread a thin layer of rice on top. Arrange 1/4 of the crabmeat, avocado, and pickled ginger over rice in a line down the centre. Lift one end of the meat and roll it tightly over filling to make a complete roll. Repeat with remaining ingredients.
  6. Use a wet, sharp knife to cut each roll into 6 to 8 slices.
